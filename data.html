Importance

• For Coastal Protection:

Coral can absorb waves energy.They reduce the damage in case of storms, hurricanes and the energy of tsunamis.

• For Habitat:

Over one million plant and animal species are involved in coral reefs and they hostreceive more than 25% of all species of marine life.

• For Food Resource:

One eighth of the world’s population live within 100 kilometres of coral and a large proportion of food taken directly from the reef waters.

Threats

Regardless of global threats to coral reef such as ocean acidification, increased ocean temperatures and changing ocean chemistry. Coral reefs are particularly vulnerable to the effects of human activities, both through direct exploitation of reef resources, and through indirect impacts from human activities.
